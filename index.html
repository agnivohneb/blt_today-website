About The Association

Our mission statement is "Community today for tomorrow". To us that means building a strong community now, in the present, to make us stronger for whatever comes tomorrow. Without the community spirit, we don't engage with the people we live near. Without engagement, we just live here.

In 2015, six local residents volunteered their time to plan the very first BLT Canada Day Celebration. With help of sponsorships, they were able to provide everything at the event for free to the community, meaning the community could enjoy Canada Day without the usual costs to attend any celebration in Halifax. The next year these six residents formed the group BLT Today and registered the name.

Now the BLT Today core group continues to plan and put on Canada Day Celebrations each year, and with help from sponsors we continue to provide this function free to the community. A few sub-comities have formed over the years for other events like the Tree Lighting.

FAQ

The most common questions we get and you are probably thinking. Did we not answer it here? Then send us a message.

When is the event?

Sunday July 1st, 2018 from 8:30 am to 10:30 am for the Pancake Breakfast and 6:30 pm to 9:30 pm for the Carnival. Fireworks will take place at 9:40 pm.

Where are the celebrations happening?

On the properties of Timberlea Dental and D & Jo’s Country Market at 1823 St. Margaret’s Bay Road.

What if it rains?

Should weather alter the schedule of the event, BLT Today will post information on Facebook.

How much does it cost?

The entertainment, food, games and fun are all completely free, sponsored by local businesses.

What do I bring?

You should bring friends, family, your Canadian spirit and perhaps a chair. There will be 50/50 tickets available, $2 each or 3 for $5 should you wish to participate in the draw.

Can I bring my pet?

For the comfort and safety of both pets and community members, we ask you to allow your pets to stay home where they are safe.

Where do we park?

If you choose to drive instead of walking or taking Metro Transit, there is very limited parking available in the Guardian parking lot and along neighboring side roads. Please do not park in no parking zones, at the Ultramar or Tim Hortons.

Is this just for families or young children?

Absolutely not. There is something for everyone. There will be live music, bouncy castles, obstacle course, a dunk tank, face painting, Jimmy J, midway games and food.

Will there be food?

This year’s Canada Day theme is a carnival theme, so there will be cotton candy, popcorn, hot dogs, cake, and ice cream.

What can I do to help?

BLT Today needs volunteers to run and event like this one so please take a moment to sign up as a volunteer at blttoday.ca/volunteer.html.
